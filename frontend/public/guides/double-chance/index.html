How double chance works

Double chance combines two results in a single bet: home or draw (1X), away or draw (X2), or home or away (12).

Because you cover two outcomes, odds are lower than a standard 1X2 bet.

When to use it

  • When you expect a tight match and want protection against a draw.
  • When backing an underdog but still want cover on the favorite.
  • When you want safer odds for accumulators.

FAQ

Is double chance safer?

It reduces risk by covering two outcomes, but the odds are lower than a single result.

What does 12 mean in double chance?

It means home win or away win, so the only losing result is a draw.

Do all bookmakers offer double chance?

Most major Ghana and Nigeria bookmakers include double chance markets for popular leagues.
